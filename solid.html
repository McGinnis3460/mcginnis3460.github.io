Eastern Wormsnake


The scientific name for the Eastern Wormsnake is Carphophis amoenus amoenus.

Hard to find and are found underground in wood and fields. Also under logs, tree bark, boards, rocks, trash, andflat stones.the are also found in wet logs and stumps.

Habitats for the Eastern Wormsnake is widesptead through out the entire state of Virginia.

Northen Black Racer


The Northen Black Racer has an solid adult and a blotched juvenile stage.

The scientific name for the Northen Black Racer is Coluber constrictor constrictor.

Found in fields, meadows, forests, dry areas away from moisture, grasslands,mountains,under logs, used boards, car hoods, and other large types of surface trash.This snake is terrestrial.

Habitats for the Northen Black Racer is widesptead through out the entire state of Virginia.

Northern Ring-necked Snake


The scientific for the Northern Ring-necked Snake is Diadophis punctatus edwardsii.

Hard to find but inhabit forests, where they are typicaly found under dead leaves, also under logs, rocks, boards and are sometimes found in caves.

The Nothern Ring Necked snake is usually located in the southeastern region of the state below the James River and in the middle of the state where they intermingle with the Northern Ring-Necked Snake (Diadophis punctatus punctatus).

Southern Ring-necked Snake


The scientific for the Southern Ring-necked Snake is Diadophis punctatus punctatus.

Hard to find but inhabit forests, where they are typicaly found under dead leaves, also under logs, rocks, boards, tends to avoid dry soil, and are sometimes found in caves.

The Southern Ring Necked snake is usually located in the western region of the state in the Shenendoah Valley and in the middle of the state where they intermingle with the Southern Ring-Necked Snake (Diadophis punctatus edwardsii).

Eastern Mudsnake


The scientific name for the Eastern Mudsnake is Farancia abacura abacura.

Hard to find and are found in streams, canals, swamps, forested wetlands, creeks, ponds, and lakes.

The range of this species is limited to the southeastern counties of the state.

Rough Earthsnake


The scientific name for the Rough Earthsnake is Haldea striatula.

Found under trash, construction scraps,in woods, meadows, and fields.

Also found in suburban green space.

This speicie's habitats are mostly found in the southern part of the tidewater region.

Eastern hog-nosed Snake


Hognose snake has a solid and blotched variety.

The scientific name for the Eastern hog-nosed Snake is Heterodon platirhinos.

Found in areas with sandy soil, field,meadows, forests, woods, and under surface objects.

Habitats for the Eastern hog-nosed Snake are widesptead through out the entire state of Virginia.

Northern Mole Kingsnake


The scientific name for the Northern Mole Kingsnake is Lampropeltis calligaster rhombomaculata.

Found in fields, overgrown fields, woods, forests, suburban green space, and city parks.

Habitats for the Northern Mole Kingsnake are found in the peidmont and most of the tidewater reigion and are rarely found in the blue ridge mountains.

Eastern Glossy Swampsnake


The scientific name for the Eastern Glossy Swampsnake is Liodytes rigida rigida.

This is an aquatic snake.

Hard to find and is found in saltwater marshes, on mats of grass, under ground, in ponds, canals, and streams.

Habitats are only found in Charles City county, James City county, and New Kent county.

Plain-bellied Watersnake


The scientific name for the Plain-bellied Watersnake is Nerodia erythrogaster.

Found in swamps, ditchesmarshes, streams, on and under logs, also under boards, litter and logs

Habitats are found in the southeastern corner of Virginia.

Northern Rough Greensnake


The scientific name for the Northern Rough Greensnake is Opheodrys aestivus.

Found in forests, fields, thickets, shrubs, and hedgegrows.

Habitats for the Northern Rough Greensnake are widesptead through out the entire state of Virginia except for these counties: Craig county, Giles county, Bland county, Tazewell county, Smyth county, Grayson county, and Washington county.

Smooth Greensnake


The scientific name for the Smooth Greensnake is Opheodrys vernalis.

Hard to find and is found in fields, forests, and meadows.

Habitats are found in the upper northwest section of Virginia.

Eastern Ratsnake


The Eastern Ratsnake has an solid adult and a blotched juvenile stage.

The scientific name for the Eastern Ratsnake is Pantherophis alleghaniensis.

Found in forests, urban green space, swamps, barns, old buildings, and caves.

Habitats for the Eastern Ratsnake are widesptead through out the entire state of Virginia.

Dekay's Brownsnake


The scientific name for the Dekay's Brownsnake is Storeria dekayi dekayis.

Hard to find and are found under boards, trash, logs, rocks, also in forests, woods, grasslands, and green space.

Habitats are found in most of Virginia and are hard to find in the Appalachian plateau, valley and ridge, and blue ridge mountains.

Red-bellied Snake


The scientific name for the Red-bellied Snake is Storeria occipitomaculata.

Hard to find and is found in forests, fields, green space, wetlands, under rocks, logs, boards, bark, and leaves.

Habitats are found in most of Virginia and are hard to find in the Appalachian plateau

Southeastern Crowned Snake


The scientific name for the Southeastern Crowned Snake is Tantilla coronata.

Found in forests, woods, green space, under wood, and rocks.

Habitats are hard to find and are only found in Amherst county, Campbell county, Bedford county, Pittsylvania county, Henry county, and Isle of Wight county.

Mountain Earthsnake


The scientific name for the Mountain Earthsnake is Virginia valeriae pulchra.

Found in foests, woods, ecotones, lowlands, under boards, logs, trash, leave, and straw.

Habitats are hard to find and are found in Highland county.

Eastern Smooth Earthsnake


The scientific name for the Eastern Smooth Earthsnake is Virginia valeriae valeriae.

Found in forests, woods, fields, ridges, pastures, green space, ecotones, lowlands, under boards, logs, trash, leaves, and straw.

Habitats are found throughout most of the state but very rarely in the Virginia panhandle except for Scott county.
