Northern Scarletsnake


The Northern Scarletsnake also looks blotched.

The scientific name for the Northern Scarletsnake is Cemophora coccinea copei.

Found in sandy areas, and under logs.

Habitats are found in north and southeastern corner of thepeidmont region , and the tide water region,except for Alleghany county.

Eastern Kingsnake


The scientific name for the Northern Scarletsnake is Lampropeltis getula.

Found in forests, fields, swamps, marshes, creeks, streams, under boards, logs, tar paper, and old car hoods.

Habitats are found in most of Virginia, Dickenson county, Wise county, and Russel county, except for the panhandle and Fredrick county, Highland county, Bath county, Alleghany county, and Craig county.

Scarlet Kingsnake


The Scarlet Kingsnake also looks blotched.

The scientific name for the Northern Scarletsnake is Lampropeltis triangulum elapsoides.

Found in forests, fields, swamps, marshes, creeks, streams, under boards, logs, tar paper, and old car hoods.

Habitats are found in Nelson county, Bedford county, Pittsylvania county, New Kent county, Chesterfield county,Sussex county, Southampton county, and the county of Virginia Beach

Eastern Milksnake


The Eastern Milksnake also looks blotched.

The scientific name for the Eastern Milksnake is Lampropeltis triangulum triangulum.

Hard to find and are found under rocks, logs, stumps, and boards.

Habitats for the Eastern Milksnake is widesptead through out the entire state of Virginia.
