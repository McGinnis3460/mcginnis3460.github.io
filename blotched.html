Eastern hog-nosed Snake


Hog-nose snake has a solid and blotched variety.

The scientific name for the Eastern hog-nosed Snake is Heterodon platirhinos.

Found in areas with sandy soil, field,meadows, forests, woods, and under surface objects.

Habitats for the Eastern hog-nosed Snake are widesptead through out the entire state of Virginia.

Northern Scarletsnake


The Northern Scarletsnake also looks banded.

The scientific name for the Northern Scarletsnake is Cemophora coccinea copei.

Found in sandy areas, and under logs.

Habitats are found in north and southeastern corner of thepeidmont region , and the tide water region,except for Alleghany county.

Northen Black Racer


The Northen Black Racer has an solid adult and a blotched juvenile stage.

The scientific name for the Northen Black Racer is Coluber constrictor constrictor.

Found in fields, meadows, forests, dry areas away from moisture, grasslands,mountains,under logs, used boards, car hoods, and other large types of surface trash.This snake is terrestrial.

Habitats for the Northen Black Racer is widesptead through out the entire state of Virginia.

Scarlet Kingsnake


The Scarlet Kingsnake also looks banded.

The scientific name for the Northern Scarletsnake is Lampropeltis triangulum elapsoides.

Found in forests, fields, swamps, marshes, creeks, streams, under boards, logs, tar paper, and old car hoods.

Habitats are found in Nelson county, Bedford county, Pittsylvania county, New Kent county, Chesterfield county,Sussex county, Southampton county, and the county of Virginia Beach

Habitats are found in most of the ceter of Virginia and is rare in the panhandle and the extreme eastern coast.

Northern Pinesnake


The scientific name for the Northern Pinesnake is Pituophis melanoleucus melanoleucus.

Hard to find burrowing snakes. When they are found it is uysually in rocky, dry moutain slopes and ridges. They prefer areas with a great amount of rock cover. The soil is typically sandy to allow for the construction of burrows.

Habitats are hard to find and are found in Augusta county, Bath county, Botetourt county, and Craig county.
